# Priorities Framework

## Overview
This document defines your personal priority framework for task prioritization. Customize this to match how you evaluate importance and urgency.

## Priority Categories

### Current Framework: Eisenhower Matrix

**Quadrant 1: Do First (Urgent & Important)**
Characteristics:
- Deadlines today or tomorrow
- Blocking other people's work
- Critical business impact
- Emergency situations

Examples:
- Production bug affecting customers
- Deadline due today
- Client crisis
- Critical system outage

Action: Do these first, protect this time fiercely.

---

**Quadrant 2: Schedule (Important, Not Urgent)**
Characteristics:
- Strategic projects
- Long-term value
- Professional development
- Relationship building
- Preventing future problems

Examples:
- Quarterly planning
- Learning new skills
- Team building activities
- Process improvements
- Health checkups

Action: Schedule dedicated time blocks for these.

---

**Quadrant 3: Delegate (Urgent, Not Important)**
Characteristics:
- Demands immediate attention
- Low long-term value
- Can be done by others
- Routine admin
- Interruptions

Examples:
- Routine email responses
- Scheduling meetings
- Data entry
- Some reports
- Non-critical requests

Action: Delegate when possible, batch together, minimize.

---

**Quadrant 4: Eliminate (Neither Urgent nor Important)**
Characteristics:
- No meaningful value
- Time wasters
- Busywork
- Excessive meetings
- Unnecessary processes

Examples:
- Excessive social media
- Unnecessary meetings
- Perfectionism on minor details
- Checking email constantly

Action: Eliminate or drastically reduce.

## Custom Priority Indicators

### High Priority Keywords
Tasks containing these words get priority boost:
- **Deadline indicators**: due, deadline, today, tomorrow, urgent, asap
- **Impact keywords**: critical, important, revenue, client, customer
- **Blockers**: blocked, blocking, dependency, prerequisite
- **Visibility**: presentation, demo, review, stakeholder

### Low Priority Keywords
Tasks with these may be deprioritized:
- **Optional**: if time, whenever, eventually, someday
- **Low impact**: nice to have, would be good, consider
- **Future**: later, next quarter, next year

## Category Weights

### Work Tasks
- Weight: 1.2 (20% higher priority)
- Rationale: Professional commitments have higher stakes
- Auto-flag: Client-facing, revenue-related, team dependencies

### Personal Tasks  
- Weight: 1.0 (baseline)
- Rationale: Life maintenance is important
- Auto-flag: Health, family, finances, home

### Side Project Tasks
- Weight: 0.9 (10% lower priority)
- Rationale: Growth activities but not urgent
- Auto-flag: Learning, ARDEN improvements, experiments

## Priority Overrides

- [ ] Health emergencies

### Always Low Priority
These should rarely take precedence:

- [ ] Fear of missing out (FOMO) activities

## Personal Values Alignment

### Top 3 Values (priority drivers)
1. **Family & Health**: Well-being comes first
2. **Professional Excellence**: Deliver quality work
3. **Continuous Growth**: Always learning and improving

### Alignment Check
When prioritizing, ask:
- Does this align with my top 3 values?
- Will I regret doing or not doing this?
- Does this move me toward my goals?

## Decision Framework

### When Everything is Urgent
Use this tie-breaker order:
1. Deadlines today/tomorrow
2. Blocking other people's work
3. High visibility/high impact
4. Quick wins (< 15 minutes)
5. Energy fit (match to current energy)

### The "Hell Yeah or No" Test
If it's not a "hell yeah, I should do this", it's a no.
Applies especially to Q3 and Q4 tasks.

### 10-10-10 Rule
When deciding priority, consider impact:
- In 10 minutes, how will I feel?
- In 10 months, how will I feel?
- In 10 years, how will I feel?

## Weekly Priority Reset

### Sunday Evening Ritual
Review and reset priorities for the week:
- [ ] Review last week's completions
- [ ] Identify week's big 3 goals
- [ ] Block time for Q2 activities
- [ ] Clear Q4 items from list
- [ ] Prepare for week ahead
